Overview

The Computer Misuse Act 1990 is a UK law that makes it a criminal offense to perform certain activities with computers or computer systems. The Act aims to protect computer systems and data from unauthorized access and misuse. It has been amended several times to keep up with technological advancements and changing threats. (Source: Legislation.gov.uk)

Key Aspects

  • Unauthorized Access: It is an offense to gain unauthorized access to computer material. (Source: Legislation.gov.uk Section 1)
  • Unauthorized Access with Intent to Commit Further Offenses: It is an offense to gain unauthorized access to computer material with the intent to commit or facilitate the commission of further offenses. (Source: Legislation.gov.uk Section 2)
  • Unauthorized Modification of Computer Material: It is an offense to perform any unauthorized act with intent to impair the operation of any computer, prevent or hinder access to any program or data held in any computer, or impair the operation of any such program or the reliability of any such data. (Source: Legislation.gov.uk Section 3)
